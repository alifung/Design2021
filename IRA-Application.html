Online IRA
Application

As someone who has been recently left their job, I want to roll over my 401K into an IRA so that I can continue contributing to my account.

Kelly Sudovar (Product owner)

Tools/Approach

Moderated user testing, Iterative design, Adobe XD, Agile, Lean UX

The opportunity

Provide a digital way for customers to apply for an IRA without having to speak to a representative on the phone.

The User

Jeff, 45, Software developer

$80K in Savings | $100K in Retirement Accounts | $240K in Annual Income
Jeff is mostly concerned with growing his retirement Accesses his finances mostly through his phone (ApplePay, Robinhood) but will use his computer for serious transactions Distrustful of in-person interactions, feels that he is able to self-educate in most situations

“It’s 2021, if I’m able to order groceries from the seat of my couch without talking to a human, I should be able to roll over my 401K completely online.”

Motivations

01

Get through the entire process without having to talk to a human

02

03

USER JOURNEY

Ideation and validation

Low fidelity research findings

01

Customers wanted to feel educated and confident in the process of rolling over their 401K.

02

Customers were comfortable with entering information, as long as they knew why they needed to provide it.

03

Customers were scared that they would roll their money over wrong, and wanted assurance they they weren’t accidentally setting themselves up for paying unecessary taxes.

Opening an IRA user flow

Iterations and testing

As I began incorporating and building upon existing design systems, I tested the patterns that would be used enterprise-wide. Customers found the UI to be clean and no-frills, but also found the experience to be very text-heavy.

Learnings

Low fidelity research findings

01

Iterating and designing with non-designers opens the door to infinitely better solutions. Running workshops and sessions with my developers helped make sure everyone was on the same page.

02

Customer centricity was key to knowing what we needed to deliver. Hearing directly from the customers helped us determine what features were critical for launch, and which ones could be added to the backlog.

03

Tools that would help customers fill in information like their tax bracket could increase confidence along the journey.
