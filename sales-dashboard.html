Sales Dashboard

As a life insurance agent, I want to quickly see my book of business so that I can reach my sales goals with confidence.

April 2020

Contributors

Dayoung Mun (UX/UI), Tatiana Kligerman (UX/UI)

Tools/Approach

Tools/Techniques: In-person interviews, ideation workshops, data comprehension, Sketch

Discovery

The opportunity

Create a digital experience for various members of the life insurances sales process to track applications, increase communication between different players, and enable realtime tracking of performance against sales goals.

Journey Map

Business impact

Providing a positive experience for third party agents would encourage them to sell our products, and the success of this platform could easily be scaled to other Latin American countries.

The Users

Beatriz, 34, Wholesaler

Bea has been working as a wholesaler for 4 years, and before that she was a protection consultant. She spends most of her time on the road, visiting her protection consultants and giving them the facetime that lets them know she’s there for them.

“What really helps me reach my sales goal each quarter is focusing on building a relationship with my protection consultants.”

Motivations

01

Meet and exceed her sales goals so that she can increase her commission

02

Support her protection consultants throughout the sales journey

03

Provide a great experience to the end customer so her protection consultants are more likely to sell more of her products

Testing
and
Validation

To validate our designs, we had the opportunity to bring wholesalers from Brazil to New Jersey to interview them. We had sketching sessions and frequent phone conversations to run them through our designs and gather feedback. For the dashboard, we spent hours understanding how the current visualizations help wholesalers and agents determine what actions to take next.

Findings

01

Bea really needed a mobile experience because she was always traveling to visit her protection consultants.

02

At a glance, the most important thing to Bea was whether she was on track to meeting her goal or not. Her trajectory for the quarter was the primary driver of any next action she would take.

03

Seeing her issued cases was important, but Bea wanted to see her pending cases, since those were the ones that she could press the backoffice to speed up if she was in a rush to reach her monthly goals.

The Solution

Over 60 screens were designed and built within salesforce to provide protection consultants, wholesalers, and executives with the digital solutions to meet their needs. Due to technological restraints within the platform, what’s shown below is the design that would have best solved for Bea’s needs and not what the finished product looked like. Due to technology limitations, the final product looked very different than what was proposed.

Looking Back

This was one example of a designer’s worst nightmare; designing something that can’t be built. Why did this happen? We designed (and promised) something without involving, collaborating, and co-creating with technology. Take it from me; there’s no better way to learn a lesson than seeing unsatisfied customers.

Learnings

01

Include ALL team members (design, technology, and product) at the inception and at along every step of the project.

02

Never promise a product you can’t deliver. Promising and not delivering breaks trust with key members that you may never get back.

03

Face-to-face time matters. In this project, I worked with a team in California along with customers in Latin America. The most effective times in the project were when we were all in the same room together; developers, customers, designers, and product managers.
