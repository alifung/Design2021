Call Center Optimization

As a call center counselor, I need to focus on finding the right solutions for customers to meet my sales goals.

April 2020

Tools/Approach

Ethnographic studies, service design, sales enablement

Discovery

The Opportunity

Improve retirement account sales by addressing current struggles that representatives were facing and propose methods to improve workflow and the ability for representatives to service customers.

INTERVIEWING REPRESENTATIVES AT A CALL CENTER IN MN

Key Pain Points

01

The representatives had too many systems to jump back and forth between to get basic information.

02

Each representative had their own approach to the call, leaving an inconsistent experience and variable performance between representatives

03

Documents were poorly organized and inconsistent in format, making it difficult for representatives to sort through them to find the answer.

The Impact

Give retirement counselors the support they need to retain more assets from customers whose retirement plans with Prudential are being terminated. For a company known for life insurance, improving the experience of customer interactions with call counselors could help solidify the company as a trusted retirement provider.

Approach

To familiarize the team with the call center experience, we had weekly call listening sessions where we would listen to a randomly chosen call. I also shadowed several representatives on and off the phones,  conducted surveys, and hosted one-on-one interviews.

The Users

Winston, 36, Retirement Counselor

Winston has been working at Prudential as a retirement counselor for eight years now. He genuinely loves talking to people, but he feels that the various systems and processes he has to adhere to take way too much of him time up.

“My favorite part of the job is talking to people -- I only wish I had more time to spend doing it.”

Motivations

Help customers find a retirement solution that fits their needs
‍
Reach his sales goals so that he can receive commission
‍
Establish trust and build rapport with clients

Sales support solutions

Helping representatives build their skills

Our representatives desperately needed brushing up on their skills to get to their ambitious sales goal.

After many conversations and ideation sessions with the call center managers, the representatives, and our leaders, we developed a series of solutions to address the skill development our team needed.  

Bi-weekly Huddles for knowledge-Sharing

Huddles would allow reps to quickly set their minds on the goal at hand, share best practices, and inform leadership of roadblocks that needed to be addressed.

“It’s so great to hear from your peers, especially when we’re all dealing with the same issues, it’s nice to know how other people deal with it”
‍
- Retirement counselor after the first week of huddles

Using Customer Feedback to grow

Post-call customer surveys would allow reps to see how they were perceived by customers. Although reps weren’t having trouble being kind and building rapport with customers, we felt it was important that they hear what their customers thought of the interactions they had with our reps.

“Winston was a pleasure to speak to, but I only wish I could remember what he said so I could tell my husband. I understand him when we’re on the phone, but a few hours later I can’t remember everything.”

- Ana, a customer of 14 years

Evaluating Progress

To begin evaluating the efficacy of our work, we launched a survey that we sent to the representatives with the following results:

25%

felt that the huddles positively improved communication on the team

60%

said they felt empowered and received the support they needed to reach their sales goal

Inefficiencies

The Problem

For a simple call where a participant decides to keep their funds in their Prudential plan, a rep spends:

10 minutes

getting a full picture of the customer’s profile and assets

8 minutes post-call

documenting and logging the call in all the appropriate systems

25 minutes post-call

attaching and sending regulatory paperwork

To encourage and facilitate IRA sales, we had to make it easier and quicker for representatives to do their job and move on to their next sale. Every minute spent off the phone was lost time that could have been spent building trust and rapport with the next customer.

Future state journey map

After many conversations with our representatives, technology teams, and regulatory groups, we crafted a future state journey map that we used to build out the backlog. With low fidelity wireframes, we were quickly able to move to using salesforce components to design a system optimized for the representative’s call flow, with everything at the touch of their fingers.
